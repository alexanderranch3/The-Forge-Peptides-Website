About Retatrutide

Retatrutide (development code LY3437943) is a synthetic trispecific peptide that engages three receptors at once — the GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon receptors. This simultaneous multi-receptor engagement is what sets it apart from the single- and dual-agonist peptides that preceded it, and it is the reason the compound has become a focal point of recent metabolic research literature.

In the laboratory, Retatrutide is studied for its role in energy balance, adipose reduction pathways, and glycemic regulation. Investigators use in-vitro and preclinical models to examine how coordinated signaling across the GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon axes behaves relative to compounds that act on only one or two of those receptors. All work with this material at The Forge is intended strictly for in-vitro laboratory research and never for human or animal use.

Retatrutide is offered in four vial sizes — 10mg, 12mg, 15mg, and 24mg — so a research group can match vial quantity to the scale of a given study rather than over-ordering or splitting material across vials. Every vial is lyophilized and independently third-party lab tested at ≥98% purity by HPLC, and a Certificate of Analysis for any batch is available on request by email. Store lyophilized vials cool, dry, and out of direct light; refrigeration is recommended for longer-term storage, and repeated temperature swings should be avoided to preserve peptide integrity.
